Summary of Analyses

Cannabis

Cannabis use showed strong associations with education and country. Higher-education groups (Master‚Äôs, University, Professional diploma) had the highest usage. The USA and UK showed the highest usage rates.

Users scored higher in Openness and Neuroticism and lower in Agreeableness and Conscientiousness. Frequency of cannabis use was positively correlated with Openness and negatively with Conscientiousness and Agreeableness.

Nicotine

Nicotine use was highest among respondents with partial college, professional certificates, or lower education, while those with advanced degrees used least. The USA and UK again exhibited strong usage patterns.

Nicotine users had lower Conscientiousness and Agreeableness and higher Neuroticism. Usage frequency showed similar correlations.

Amphetamines

Amphetamine use varied significantly by both education and country, with elevated use in the USA, UK, and Canada.

Amphetamine users showed the strongest personality separation: much higher Openness and Neuroticism and markedly lower Conscientiousness and Agreeableness.

Benzodiazepines

Benzodiazepine users tended to have lower Conscientiousness and Agreeableness, and higher Neuroticism. Usage varied by country, with the USA and UK displaying elevated prevalence.

Frequency of benzo use correlated negatively with Conscientiousness and Agreeableness, and positively with Neuroticism.

Stimulants (Cocaine + Methamphetamine)

Stimulant use showed meaningful differences across education and country. Users tended to be less conscientious and less agreeable, with elevated Neuroticism and Openness.

Usage frequency correlated strongly with Openness and Neuroticism.

High Risk (Heroin + Crack + VSA)

Though less common overall, high-risk drug use displayed distinct patterns: users showed markedly lower Conscientiousness and Agreeableness, paired with substantially elevated Neuroticism.

Usage was geographically clustered, with higher concentrations in the USA and UK.

Psychedelics (LSD + Mushrooms + Ketamine + Ecstasy + Legal-H)

Psychedelic users were more likely to have university-level education and reported higher Openness scores than non-users. Usage frequency correlated positively with Openness and Neuroticism and negatively with Conscientiousness.

Country-level differences were also significant, with higher prevalence in North America and Europe.

Interpretation

Across the analyzed substances‚Äîcannabis, nicotine, amphetamines, benzodiazepines, stimulants, psychedelics, and high-risk drugs‚Äîconsistent psychological themes emerged.

Openness was a strong positive predictor of both likelihood and frequency of use. Conscientiousness showed a robust negative relationship with use across nearly all categories. Neuroticism was especially elevated among stimulant, benzodiazepine, and high-risk users.

Sociodemographic context (education level, country) also shaped usage patterns. While no causal claims can be made, the framework of personality √ó environment provides a coherent explanation for the observed patterns.
