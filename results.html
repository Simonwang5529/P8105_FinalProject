Summary of Analyses

  • Chi-square tests revealed significant associations between education level and use of all three drugs (Cannabis, Nicotine, Amphetamines), as well as strong country-level differences in usage patterns.
  • ANOVA tests showed meaningful personality differences between users and non-users. Cannabis users displayed higher Openness, nicotine users showed lower Conscientiousness, and amphetamine users showed elevated Neuroticism.

Interpretation

Across all three substances, personality traits demonstrated clear and consistent relationships with both the likelihood of use and the intensity of use. Individuals higher in Openness were more likely to report cannabis use and to use it more frequently. Conscientiousness showed a negative association with nicotine use, suggesting more habitual or impulsive patterns among smokers. Neuroticism was elevated among amphetamine users and increased with usage frequency.

These results highlight stable psychological profiles associated with different forms of substance use. While the findings do not imply causation, they align with existing behavioral research and suggest personality plays a meaningful role in shaping both experimentation and habitual patterns of drug use.
